CHAI@IJCAI-ECAI 2022

We hosted the 1st Workshop on Communication in Human-AI Interaciton, at the IJCAI-ECAI 2022 Conference!

Jennifer Renoux
Aug 1, 2022 Workshop
Image credit: IJCAI-22

On July 24, 2022, we hosted the first workshop on Communication in Human-AI Interaction (CHAI) at the 31st International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence (IJCAI 2022). The goal of this one-day workshop was to bring together experts from AI, HCI, and Cognitive Sciences to explore and understand the specificities and characteristics of communication in human-AI interactions, as well as the salient principles, methods, and theories one has to consider to build meaningful human-AI communication systems.

We attended 8 paper presentations, as well as two very inspiring keynotes.

Nicolas Fay, Associate Professor of Cognitive Sciences, presented to us the Role of Iconicity and Embodiment to the Evolution of Human Communication.

Mark Riedl, Professor in Artificial Intelligence, argumed for Automated Story Generation as a Lens for Fundamental Artificial Intelligence.

We were very glad to have a very good attendance and fantastic discussions. Thanks to the organizing team, the PC members, and all the participants for making this event a success!

The link to the accepted papers and the slides of our keynotes are available on the workshop website.

Stay tuned, for a 2023 edition might be coming! 😉
